8. Grammar Focus: Describing Changes

Verb + Adverb Combinations

When describing trends and changes, we often use a verb (increase, decrease, etc.) + an adverb (sharply, slightly, etc.)

Sales increased dramatically in the third quarter.
Profit fell significantly after the new regulations.
Customer satisfaction grew steadily throughout the year.
Production costs remained relatively stable despite inflation.

Practice Time

Create sentences about business trends using these combinations:

  • revenue + increase + gradually
  • market share + grow + consistently
  • expenses + rise + unexpectedly
  • employee turnover + decrease + substantially
  • customer engagement + improve + steadily

11. Reading: The Data Revolution in Business

Read the following text about the importance of data in modern business:

The Data-Driven Decision Makers

In the 21st century, data has become the most valuable asset for businesses across all industries. Companies like Amazon, Google, and Netflix have demonstrated how strategic data analysis can transform entire markets and create unprecedented competitive advantages.

Modern organizations collect and analyze data from multiple sources: customer interactions, social media, website traffic, sales records, and even IoT devices. This comprehensive approach allows businesses to:

  • Predict customer behavior with remarkable accuracy
  • Optimize operational efficiency
  • Develop personalized marketing strategies
  • Identify emerging market trends
  • Make informed strategic decisions

However, the true power of data lies not just in collection, but in interpretation. Successful companies employ data scientists, statisticians, and business analysts who can transform raw numbers into actionable insights.

Comprehension Questions

  • What makes data so valuable in modern business?
  • List three sources of data mentioned in the text.
  • Why is interpretation more important than mere collection?
  • How might different professionals use data in an organization?

13. Ethical Debate: Data Privacy vs. Business Innovation

Divide into two groups and prepare arguments for the following debate motion:

"In the pursuit of business innovation, companies should have unrestricted access to user data."
Group A: Proposition (Support the Motion)

Argue that data access drives innovation, improves customer experiences, and creates economic value.

Group B: Opposition (Against the Motion)

Argue that unrestricted data access violates individual privacy rights and poses significant ethical risks.

Debate Preparation

  • Research real-world examples
  • Prepare statistical and ethical arguments
  • Use clear, respectful language
  • Be prepared to respond to counterarguments
